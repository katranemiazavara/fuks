While ventilation and air quality primarily depend on the facility manager or building operator, occupants have 5 tools to help clean the air and ensure it is acceptable.

Windows and Doors

This is the ventilation tool everyone can have in their room. Even windows being cracked open slightly can have an improvement on air quality.

Air Movement

One of the more common problems is that the ventilation system is not on at all (broken fan) or that it is set to turn off periodically during the day. In schools, offices or other commercial settings, this should never happen. Ventilation should always be on. You can always tape a tissue onto a meter stick or broom handle and hold it up to the diffuser to watch it flap. A better solution is to tape it there permanently so you can always check it without any effort.

Thermostat

This is very common in strip malls and many other settings. The unit supplying air to the space has two functions — ventilation (supplying outdoor air) and climate control (heating and cooling). Putting the fan in auto mode means the unit only turns on if heating or cooling are necessary. If heating or cooling are not necessary, the unit will turn off and the ventilation will stop. You want the ventilation to be on all the time, so put the fan setting in On. At night, you don’t need to have ventilation in an unoccupied space, so turn it to Auto.

For your home, your furnace generally doesn’t have ventilation unless you have a ERV attached. If you are using your furnace to clean the air in your home, then also leave the fan on. For most people, it can be left in auto.

Carbon Dioxide

CO2 is the best tool occupants have to verify if the ventilation is working. Classrooms should remain under 1000 ppm if they are complying with minimum ventilation requirements. If they are consistently above 1000 ppm, you can ask the facility manager if the ventilation is working properly. You can get more info about CO2 monitoring here.

HEPA Filters and Corsi-Rosenthal Box

Portable air cleaners are the cheapest and quickest tools to clean the air. For them to be effective, it’s important to understand how to solve the noise problem. For any questions you have about HEPA filters, you can see this FAQ. If you are debating between a Corsi-Rosenthal box or HEPA filter, some guidance can be found here.
